Our Story

A family, a hotel,
and thirty years
of welcomes

How it started

Hotel Mistral opened in 1990, when Alghero was still a quiet fishing town with a small airport and a reputation for clear water. Our family had lived in the area for generations. We converted a residential building into a small hotel — 25 rooms, a garden, a bar, and a belief that hospitality is something you do in person.

The first guests were mostly Italian families on summer holiday. Over the years, word spread. Irish couples started coming. Then Belgians, British, Canadians. We have had guests who return every August, who bring their children, who send postcards from home.

The hotel has been refreshed over the decades — new bathrooms, air conditioning, WiFi — but the essence has not changed. We are still here every morning. We still know most guests by name.

What we believe

We are not a luxury hotel, and we do not try to be. We are a 3-star property that takes its three stars seriously. The room will be clean. The bed will be comfortable. The shower will be hot. The staff will be kind.

We believe that the best hospitality is invisible. You should notice it only when something goes wrong — and here, things rarely do. The garden is tended. The bar is stocked. The key works. Someone is at the desk when you need them.

Alghero sells itself. Our job is to give you a quiet, cool, comfortable place to return to after a day at the beach or in the old town. Nothing more. Nothing less.

Philosophy

Three principles

01

Simplicity

We do not overcomplicate. Our rooms are furnished with what you need: a good bed, a clean bathroom, a place to work, a place to store your things. The rest is up to Alghero.

02

Consistency

Guests return because they know what to expect. The room will look like the photos. The breakfast will be good. The garden will be there. Trust is built on reliability.

03

Warmth

We are a family business, not a corporation. When you check in, you are talking to someone who owns the place. That changes everything — the advice, the care, the small kindnesses.
